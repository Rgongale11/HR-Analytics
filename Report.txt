
**Objective:**

To leverage HR Analytics to identify factors influencing employee attrition and improve retention by analyzing key metrics such as age, salary, tenure, satisfaction, 
	and department trends.

**Insights:**

1. **Age & Attrition:** Highest attrition in the 26-35 age group, indicating potential dissatisfaction and lack of growth opportunities.

2. **Salary Impact:** Employees earning <5K have the highest attrition rate. Competitive compensation is needed.

3. **Early-Stage Attrition:** High turnover during the first 1-2 years, suggesting a need for better onboarding and mentorship.

4. **Job Satisfaction:** Medium job satisfaction correlates with high attrition, highlighting the need for enhanced employee engagement.

5. **Department Trends:** Sales and R&D roles experience notable attrition, requiring tailored retention strategies.

6. **Diversity Focus:** Female employees in the 26-35 age group show higher attrition, indicating a need for flexible policies and career support.

**Recommendations:**

1. Provide clearer career progression and mentorship, especially for the 26-35 age group.

2. Revise compensation for employees earning <5K to align with industry standards.

3. Strengthen onboarding and mentorship programs for new hires to reduce early-stage attrition.

4. Implement initiatives to improve employee satisfaction, such as work-life balance and recognition programs.

5. Offer targeted support for high-turnover departments like Sales and R&D.

6. Promote gender diversity through flexible policies and career development opportunities for female employees.

**Conclusion:**

By focusing on career growth, competitive compensation, enhanced onboarding, and tailored support, the organization can reduce attrition and improve employee retention, 
	leading to a more engaged and productive workforce.
